How it works
Four steps. Permanent proof.

Your document never leaves your browser. Only its mathematical fingerprint — impossible to reverse — is stored on our server.

01
📄
Select document

Upload any file — PDF, Word, image, code. Your browser computes its SHA-256 hash locally. The file never leaves your device.

02
🔑
Sign with ML-DSA

Your browser signs the document hash using your ML-DSA-65 private key — the NIST FIPS-204 quantum-resistant signature algorithm.

03
🕐
Server timestamps

PQCServer records the exact UTC timestamp and co-signs the certificate. This creates an independent second witness to the event.

04
🔗
Permanent certificate

You receive a permanent URL — pqcserver.com/ts/xxxxxxxx. Share it with anyone. They can verify the certificate without trusting PQCServer.

What it proves
Three things, cryptographically
📅
Existence at a point in time

The document with this exact content existed at or before the recorded timestamp. It cannot have been backdated.

🔒
Integrity since signing

Any modification to the document — even a single character — produces a completely different hash, instantly invalidating the certificate.

👤
Signer identity

The ML-DSA signature links the document to a specific keypair. The signer controlled that private key at the time of signing.

Legal status note

PQCServer timestamps provide strong cryptographic proof of document existence and integrity. However, they are not currently equivalent to a Qualified Electronic Signature (QES) under EU eIDAS regulation, which requires a qualified certificate from an accredited trust service provider.

ML-DSA (NIST FIPS-204) is not yet included in the ETSI/eIDAS technical profiles for qualified signatures, but this is expected to change between 2026 and 2028 as standards bodies adopt NIST post-quantum algorithms.

PQCServer timestamps are already suitable for: business contracts between consenting parties, proof of prior art, intellectual property protection, internal compliance, and any context where cryptographic proof is accepted.

Consult a legal professional for advice on admissibility in your specific jurisdiction.
